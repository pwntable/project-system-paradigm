How ShopEZ Processes Checkout

HOW IT WORKS

The Old Way: "One-at-a-Time Checkout"

Think of a restaurant with one cashier who takes your order, walks to the kitchen, waits for the chef to cook, then walks back to hand you the food — while the entire queue waits behind you. When the restaurant is busy, customers at the back give up and leave. In tech terms: the checkout system blocks the customer's browser while sequentially calling Payment, Stock, and Database services.

The Upgraded Way: "Smart Queue with Buzzer"

Think of a fast-food restaurant with a buzzer system. You order at the counter, get a buzzer instantly, and sit down. Kitchen staff prepare your food in the background — no waiting in line. The counter is free for the next customer immediately. In tech terms: the system instantly confirms the order and queues it for background processing by dedicated staff.

Presenter's Talking Points

Why the Old System Fails at Peak Hours:
  • When one shopper's payment is slow, every shopper behind them is stuck waiting too — like a single checkout lane.
  • Under heavy traffic, the system runs out of capacity and starts dropping shoppers entirely.
  • The customer sees an error on their screen and leaves — a lost sale that's nearly impossible to recover.
Why the Upgraded System Wins:
  • Every shopper gets an instant "Order Received!" confirmation — no waiting at all.
  • Orders are safely queued, and dedicated background staff process them at a steady, reliable pace.
  • Even during the biggest rush, no shopper is turned away — 100% of sales are captured.
